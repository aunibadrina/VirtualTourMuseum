History of Car in Malaysia

Home > History of Car in Malaysia

Cars in Malaysia have a history dating back to the early 20th century, with imported vehicles initially owned by the wealthy and government officials. Local assembly plants in the 1920s marked the beginning of automotive manufacturing, while the 1983 national car project introduced Proton, a pivotal milestone in the industry.


The Timeline

History Begins Here.


1902 - First Car in Malaysia


Mercedez Simplex

The first car was allegedly a Mercedez Simplex imported into Malaysia by Eu Tong Sen in 1902, a tin baron based in Perak. Many claim that this was the reason why car plates in Perak start with an 'A', others say this was because Perak had the highest number of cars at that time, and some state that it's actually representative of its then larger population ahead of Selangor (B) and Pahang (C) at that time. In any case, Perak undoubtedly remains a key cornerstone in the brief history of cars in Malaysia!



1909 - Ford places first ad in Malaysia


Ford Motor Company of Malaya (Ford Malaya)

This would be the start of an American dominance in Malaysia's automative market By 1911, there were plans to sell at least 60 cars a year. By 1926, Ford had a plant in Singapore (then Malaya) and dominated 80% of car sales in Malaya. In 1964, the MK1 Ford Cortina was the best selling car.



1956 - British vehicles eats up market share


The Straits Times, 19 March 1957, Page 12

In 1956, Europe led the way for a new foreign cars, with British imports accounting for 65% of new vehicle sales in Malaya.



1966 - Formation of Malaysian Motor Vehicles Assemblers' Association


Formation of Malaysian Automotive Association

10 automotive firms banded together to establish the Malaysia Motor Vehicle Assemblers' Association (MVAA). Singapore and Malaysia had been aggresively competing for foreign investment and the group felt that there was a need for a common ground. In 2000, the MVAA merged with Malaya Motor Traders Assocoation to form the Malaysian Automotive Association.



1968 - First locally assembled car rolls off production line


Volvo 144

By the late 1950s, there was a strong emphasis for local production and industrialisation. The government had pushed to accelerate industrialisation through the local assembly of foreign automobiles and manufacturing of components. And in November 1968, the Volvo 144 was unveiled.



1970s - 1980s - Welcoming an era of Japanese Cars


The Business Times, 15 January 1983, Page 11

Japanese cars initially had a bit of flak due to anti-Japanese sentiment from the second world war, plus its lightweight frames and lower prices did not help their case. However by 1981, Japanese brands took 6 spots in West Malaysia's car sales numbers, dominating the top 5 leading spots.



1983 - Malaysia births first car manufacturer


Tun Dr Mahathir with Proton Saga

The Heavy Corporation of Malaysia (HICOM) went into a joint venture with then-Japanese powerhouse Mitsubishi to help integrate automative production. The first Malaysian car, the Proton Saga, was launched a few years later in 1985 and was supported by then Prime Minister Dr. Mahathir Mohamad.



1993 - A new local competitor joins


Perodua's first car, Perodua kancil

Perusahaan Otomobil Kedua Sdn. Bhd. (Perodua) was establish in 1993 in a joint venture with Daihatsu. From 1994, Perodua started to sell a compact car based on Daihatsu's Mira model. When Perodua entered the market, Proton no longer enjoyed its Gulliver-type oligopoly but both companies have continued to maintain an oligopoly from the early 2000s.



2018 - Malaysia's most expensive car plate


The Star, 24 August 2018

The special car registration number plate was 'Malaysia 1', which broke the record for the most expensive bid ever made for a single number - at a beautiful price of RM1,111,111.
